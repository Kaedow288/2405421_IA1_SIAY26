AI Business Report: AI-driven financial advisory

Introduction and Application to the world:

My favorite idea of the AI Business List 2025 was number 6, AI-driven financial advisory. AI is widely used around the world. In this case, it is used for financial data, which is a good thing for advisors who may sometimes have trouble speaking about financial issues that may be difficult to explain to customers.

Its Usefulness:

AI-driven financial advisory is useful as it gives plausible explanations about financial data and can help a great number of customers, investors and other stakeholders.

Ethical Issues:

AI driven financial advisory can pose serious risks such as data privacy, financial risk and lack of human judgement. Data privacy is a risk that allows AI to have sensitive data regarding financial information. Secondly, financial risk AI can make mistakes therefore leading to significant financial losses. Finally, dependence on AI is not advisable as there is a lack of human judgement.

Solutions:

Some proposed solutions include proper implementation of privacy policies and strong data protection. Secondly, provide clear disclaimers about investment risk and finally, include advisors for complex questions that AI may not understand.

Summary of Twelve Pillars-Chapters 4-7

Chapter 4: Acheive Your Goals

Michael invites Charlie to Mack's diner to have a conversation. During their conversation they spoke about work and gardening, which gradually rotated to Michael speaking about his wife Amy. Charlie asked Michael what were his goals and he seemed unsure of the answer. Nevertheless, he tells Micheal about Mr.Davis' favourite subject goal-setting. He tells Micheal to write down his goals and work towards them. Michael admits feeling frustrated over the years because he desparately wanted to be successful but did not know where to start.

Application to my life:

This chapter has taught to set clear, specific goals that provides motivation in life. The first step to achieving your goals is to decide what you want and then write it down. Goals should be challenging enough to require personal growth and development. I will write down my goals to make them specific and to separate them into short and long term goals while regulary treacking my progress.

Chapter 5: The Proper Use of Time

After their previous discussion on setting goals, Micheal mnetions that he is swamped with work and his family so he does not know where a goal would fit in. This statement results in Charile quoting Mr.Davis once more. He explains to Micheal that in life there are two types of pain discipline and regret and the weight of discipline is far more lighter than that of regret. Charlie states that it is better to be a master of time than a servant of time.

Application to my life:

In this chapter Jim Rohn talks about time manamgent and how to use it wisely. Invest your time in things that will benefit you and push you forward for great things. It is important to use your time efficiently because once time is wasted it cannot be regained. As a result of this, I will create a study planner that can help me to keep track of assignments and exams and limit distractions that keeps me away from completing important tasks.

Chapter 6: Surround Yourself With the Best People

Micheal learns another lesson from the twelve pillars. He is taught the importance of surrounding himself with people who will uplift him. Charile states that choosing firends that help to steer you in the right direction are those whom you should keep. His words led Michael to reflect on his friend group and determine whether they are helping him to grow or not. Charlie challenges Michael to read a complete book before coming back to him for more success secrets.

Application to my life:

Chapter 6 showed how the people around us influence our success. We should choose friends and mentors who challenge us to grow and achieve more. Therefore, I will surround myself with motivated classmates and seek out mentors in the computer science field.

Chapter 7: Be a Life-Long Learner

Continuing their conversation, Charlie explains the difference between formal and self education to Michael. He states that self-education is continuosly teaching yourself. Through it, you're able to improve and grow as a person and stand out from everyone else. Additionally, he states that that learning is the beginning of wealth, health and spiritually and for one to learn they must read books, learn from successful persons by observing them and reflecting on their experiences.

Application to my life:

This chapter distinguished between formal education and self-education. True success comes from continuous learning beyond the classroom through reading and personal development. Formal education provides a foundation, true success comes from a commitment to continous learning throughout life while self education takes place through reading books and engaging in thought-provoking discussions that maintains curiousity and a growth mindset.
